**The Importance of Patch Management to Cybersecurity**

**Patch Management and Its Contribute to System Security**

Patch management is the process of finding, obtaining, examining, and using software updates or patches to computers and systems. Patches are given by software sellers to fix bugs, correct vulnerabilities, better the performance or bring new features. Patch management is essential in securing the honesty, privacy and accessibility of systems and data in cybersecurity.

Every time a weakness is found in software, it is usually a trench in the system that can be applied to it by harmful attackers. Patches are the way the manufacturer seals that trench. If  patching is not done, those weaknesses are welcome mats, stepping on which cyberattackers can come and create disruptions.

It is like shutting the front door before going to bed. If a lock company gets a flaw in their lock and delivers a patch, but you do not install it, then the house is at risk. Patch management keeps "digital locks" updated and performs their job.

**Consequences of Failing to Apply Patches Regularly**

Not giving attention to patch management is not just harmful \- it can be disastrous. Below mentioned are the consequences if patches are delayed or ignored:

**a. Hacker Attacks**

Systems that are not patched are easy targets for cybercriminals. Harmful actors routinely check for known weaknesses across networks. If a system is unpatched, they can take the upper hand of that weakness, generally with freely accessible tools to get unverified access. For example during the 2017 Equifax breach, hackers took advantage of an unpatched Apache Struts vulnerability. As a result sensitive data of more than 140 million Americans was leaked.

**b. Ransomware Attacks**

Most ransomware attacks begin with an unpatched software getting misused. Attackers misuse system vulnerabilities to breach, encode information, and persuade it to be reclaimed by payments. 

**c. Data Leakages and Legal Consequences**

Unpatched systems can cause data leakages, resulting in huge monetary loss, legal vulnerability, and damage of reputation. It is compulsory for organizations under laws such as GDPR, HIPAA, or PCI-DSS to secure their systems, routine patching is a part of it.

**d. Operational Disruption**

Unpatched systems have more possibility of breaking down, getting affected with malware, and getting offline. This, not just disrupts productivity, but also brings in losses and customer disappointment.

**Best Practices for an Effective Patch Management Strategy**

A good patch management plan doesn't come instantaneously, it's made by a combination of sound procedures, strategizing, and the proper instruments. Here are some best ways to practice good patch management:

**a. Maintain an Inventory of All Assets**

One shall not be able to patch something that he/she doesn't know they have. Keep an accurate record of all the hardware, software, and operating systems used. This helps in the identification of which systems need patching and at what moment.

**b. Prioritize Patches Based on Risk**

All patches are not made the same way. Designate & categorize patches that mark critical vulnerabilities, precisely those which are taken advantage of, wrongfully. Use the Common Vulnerability Scoring System to decide the seriousness.

**c. Test Before Deployment**

Patch implementation without checking may lead to system breakdowns or disruptions. It is always better to try the patches in an arranged environment before using them throughout the organization to prevent disruption in business activities.

**d. Automate Where Possible**

Patching by hand takes lots of time and is susceptible to mistakes. Use automated patch management instruments to make it effective and the same throughout the systems.

**e. Create a Patch Schedule**

Apply an uniform patch process, for example, monthly "Patch Tuesdays" to detect, try, and use updates. Also, make a blueprint for independent patches produced in response to complex weaknesses.

**f. Monitor and Audit**

Detecting patches turns out to check the conformity. Routine audits help in finding missed out systems and calculating the efficiency of patching.

**g. Educate Staff**

Patches are occasionally held up due to user ignorance or doubts. Make the employees understand the need for software patches and how they add value to cybersecurity.

**Risks of Unpatched Systems** 

**Cyberattacks:** An easy entry point for attackers, the weaknesses that are kept unpatched.

**Data loss:** Confidential data is accessed, misused or altered.

**Downtime:** The systems do not stay stable or appropriate for usability.

**Damage to reputation:** The trust that is destroyed and difficult to be rebuilt.

**Legal sanctions:** Not adhering with laws can result in huge penalties.

**Benefits of Keeping Software Up to Date**

**Greater security:** Patches cover the security holes that are known.

**Faster performance:** Most patches have bug fixages and performance improvements.

**Regulatory compliance:** Helps in obeying data security and cybersecurity protocols.

**Less downtime:** Updated systems breakdown less.

**Customer trust:** Show the effort to protect user information.
